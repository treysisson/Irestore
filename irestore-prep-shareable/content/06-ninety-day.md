## Phase 1: Listen & Learn (Weeks 1-2)
<!-- id: ninety-learn -->

<p class="muted">First priority is understanding the business from the inside — data, people, and processes that aren't visible from the outside.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Immerse in the data</strong> — P&L deep dive, unit economics by channel, return rate breakdown, CAC/LTV by product line, inventory positions</li>
  <li><strong>Meet every team lead 1:1</strong> — understand their priorities, pain points, and what's blocking them. Listen more than talk.</li>
  <li><strong>Map the operational reality</strong> — current processes, tools, handoffs, and where things break. Walk the warehouse. Sit with CX.</li>
  <li><strong>Understand Kevin's decision-making rhythm</strong> — how he wants to work with a COO vs. how he worked with Daniel. What "autonomous problem-solver" means in practice.</li>
  <li><strong>Identify the 3 departures' impact</strong> — Daniel (Chief of Staff, out end of April), CX Director, and Product Manager. What falls through the cracks when they leave?</li>
</ul>

---

## Phase 2: Triage & Stabilize (Weeks 3-6)
<!-- id: ninety-triage -->

<p class="muted">Address the most urgent gaps created by simultaneous departures while the business is growing 65%+ YoY.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>CX restructuring</strong> — 25-person team viewed as bloated. Implement AI-assisted workflows (call analysis, automated responses, escalation routing). This is why the previous director was let go — demonstrate the AI-first approach immediately.</li>
  <li><strong>Daniel's transition</strong> — absorb critical functions, identify what was "just messaging" vs. genuinely load-bearing. 60-90 day overlap to capture institutional knowledge.</li>
  <li><strong>Q2 launch support</strong> — skincare wand, body panels, red light belt all targeting Q2. Ensure supply chain, fulfillment, and CX are ready for 3 simultaneous product launches on top of 65% organic growth.</li>
  <li><strong>Return rate deep dive</strong> — currently 10% DTC, 14-15% Amazon, 30% Costco. All calls recorded + transcribed but NO AI analysis. Build the analytics pipeline to understand why people return.</li>
</ul>

---

## Phase 3: Build & Accelerate (Weeks 7-12)
<!-- id: ninety-build -->

<p class="muted">With the foundation stabilized, start building the systems and processes that enable the next phase of growth.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Operational dashboards</strong> — real-time visibility into the metrics that matter: revenue by channel, return rates, CX queue depth, inventory levels, ad spend efficiency. Replace spreadsheet-driven reporting.</li>
  <li><strong>International kickoff</strong> — initiate Australia TGA registration (quick win) and South Korea MFDS scoping in parallel. Identify distributor partners for Korea.</li>
  <li><strong>Supply chain optimization</strong> — demand planning for $148M+ run rate with 6-7 active product launches. Coordinate between La Mirada warehouse and multiple Shenzhen manufacturers.</li>
  <li><strong>QVC launch preparation</strong> — final diligence greenlit, prepare operational infrastructure for live commerce channel.</li>
  <li><strong>Weekly L10 rhythm</strong> — implement a lightweight operating cadence (EOS-inspired) to drive accountability, surface issues early, and keep Kevin informed without being a bottleneck.</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip"><strong>Note:</strong> This is a working sketch based on what I know from our conversations. The actual priorities will be shaped by what I learn during the onsite and the first two weeks on the ground. Some of these will be wrong — that's expected. The important thing is having a framework to organize around, not a rigid plan.</div>

## What I Don't Know Yet
<!-- id: ninety-unknowns -->

<p>Honest list of things I need to understand before any of the above becomes a real plan:</p>

<ul>
  <li>What does the P&L actually look like? Gross margins by channel, EBITDA margin, fixed vs. variable cost structure</li>
  <li>What are the real pain points that don't show up in a 2-hour conversation? What keeps the team up at night?</li>
  <li>How does decision-making actually work? What requires Kevin's approval vs. what can be run autonomously?</li>
  <li>What's the real capacity of the supply chain? Can Shenzhen handle 3 new product lines + 65% volume growth simultaneously?</li>
  <li>Where is the CX team's time actually going? 25 people handling what volume? What's automatable vs. what requires human judgment?</li>
  <li>What happened with previous COO/ops hires? What didn't work and why?</li>
</ul>
