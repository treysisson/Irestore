## Amazon Operations Framework
<!-- id: amazon-ops -->

<h3>What a COO Needs to Know About Amazon at Scale</h3>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) vs. FBM (Fulfilled by Merchant)</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>FBA:</strong> iRESTORE ships inventory to Amazon warehouses. Amazon handles pick/pack/ship + customer service. Products get Prime badge. Fees: ~15–20% total (referral 8% + FBA fees $5–7/unit for device-sized products).</li>
          <li><strong>FBM:</strong> iRESTORE ships directly from La Mirada. Lower fees (~8% referral only) but no Prime badge. Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP) is an option — keep Prime badge while shipping from your own warehouse.</li>
          <li><strong>The question to ask:</strong> "What's the current FBA/FBM split? And have you evaluated Seller Fulfilled Prime for the higher-priced devices?"</li>
          <li><strong>COO opportunity:</strong> Optimizing the FBA/FBM mix is a direct margin lever. For higher-ASP items ($600–$1,400), FBM or SFP may save $10–20/unit vs. FBA.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">AWD (Amazon Warehousing & Distribution) — The Hidden Margin Lever</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>What it is:</strong> Amazon's upstream bulk storage program. You ship pallets to AWD warehouses at lower storage rates, and Amazon automatically replenishes FBA fulfillment centers as needed. Think of it as a staging layer between your 3PL and FBA.</li>
          <li><strong>Why it matters for iRESTORE:</strong> FBA storage fees are brutal for devices — large boxes, long dwell times, and Q4 surcharges. AWD storage rates are typically <strong>50–70% cheaper</strong> than FBA storage, with no long-term storage fees and no Q4 surcharges.</li>
          <li><strong>The margin math:</strong> For a device-sized product (~$5–8/unit in FBA storage fees during peak), shifting bulk inventory to AWD and auto-replenishing FBA can save <strong>$2–4/unit in storage costs</strong>. At iRESTORE's Amazon volume, that's potentially $500K–$1M+ annually in recovered margin.</li>
          <li><strong>Additional benefits:</strong> AWD inventory counts toward your IPI score positively. Auto-replenishment reduces stockout risk during demand spikes. No per-unit fee for inbound — just storage.</li>
          <li><strong>COO opportunity:</strong> If iRESTORE is shipping everything direct to FBA, they're likely overpaying on storage. Implementing AWD as a buffer layer is a quick operational win — no customer impact, pure margin improvement. Ask: "Are you using AWD for bulk staging, or is everything going direct to FBA?"</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">IPI Score (Inventory Performance Index)</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>What it is:</strong> Amazon's score (0–1000) measuring how efficiently you manage FBA inventory. Based on: excess inventory, sell-through rate, stranded inventory, in-stock rate.</li>
          <li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> Below 400 = storage limits. Below 350 = severe restrictions. For a company with iRESTORE's volume, an IPI issue could mean they can't send enough inventory to Amazon during peak season.</li>
          <li><strong>COO action:</strong> Monitor weekly. Ensure excess inventory is removed or liquidated. Keep sell-through velocity high. This is a Brandon-level operational task that needs oversight.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Amazon PPC (Pay-Per-Click Advertising)</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Key metrics:</strong> ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sales) = ad spend ÷ ad revenue. TACoS (Total ACoS) = ad spend ÷ total revenue (including organic). The gap between ACoS and TACoS shows brand strength.</li>
          <li><strong>At iRESTORE's scale:</strong> They're likely spending $X M/year on Amazon PPC. Target ACoS for beauty devices is typically 15–25%. At their market share, they may be able to reduce without losing position.</li>
          <li><strong>COO opportunity:</strong> PPC efficiency is a profit lever. Better keyword segmentation, negative keyword management, dayparting, and bid optimization can reduce ACoS by 2–5pp. At their scale, each point matters.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Long-Term Storage Fees & Seasonal Planning</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>The trap:</strong> Amazon charges $6.90/cubic foot for inventory stored 271–365 days, and surcharges for 365+ days. For devices that take up significant space, this adds up fast.</li>
          <li><strong>Q4 planning:</strong> Amazon raises storage fees from October–December. You need to pre-position inventory by September. But overshipping = long-term storage fees. Undershipping = lost sales during peak.</li>
          <li><strong>The math:</strong> With 60–90 day Shenzhen lead times + 30 day ocean freight, Q4 inventory decisions happen in May–June. This is why demand planning matters so much.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

## Amazon Growth Levers
<!-- id: amazon-growth -->

<h3>Opportunities a COO Can Drive</h3>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">1. Listing Optimization</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>A+ Content / Brand Store:</strong> iRESTORE likely has A+ content, but is it optimized? Periodic refresh (new images, updated claims, video content) can improve conversion rate 3–10%.</li>
          <li><strong>SEO (Amazon Search):</strong> Keyword research for backend search terms. Amazon's algorithm weights title, bullet points, and backend keywords differently. Fresh keyword research every quarter.</li>
          <li><strong>Virtual bundles:</strong> Bundle hair device + serum + supplements at a slight discount. Increases AOV, improves BSR, and creates a competitive moat (harder to comparison-shop).</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">2. Amazon-to-DTC Bridge</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>The strategic goal:</strong> Every Amazon customer who becomes a DTC repeat buyer improves margins (no Amazon fees on repeat purchases). This is the single biggest margin optimization lever.</li>
          <li><strong>Tactics:</strong> Product inserts with QR codes (compliance-friendly — can't direct to DTC, but can offer warranty registration, exclusive content, or loyalty program). Post-purchase email if opted in.</li>
          <li><strong>Subscribe & Save:</strong> For consumables (serums, supplements), Amazon Subscribe & Save locks in recurring revenue and prevents competitive switching.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">3. International Amazon Marketplaces</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Amazon Global Selling:</strong> US sellers can expand to UK, Germany, Japan, Australia, Canada with relatively low friction. FBA handles local fulfillment.</li>
          <li><strong>Priority markets:</strong> Amazon UK → Amazon DE → Amazon Japan (see International section for regulatory requirements).</li>
          <li><strong>Quick wins:</strong> Amazon Canada and Amazon Australia are the lowest-friction entries from a US base.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">4. Amazon Vendor Central vs. Seller Central</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Current (likely):</strong> iRESTORE is probably on Seller Central (3P) — they control pricing, listings, and fulfillment.</li>
          <li><strong>Vendor Central (1P):</strong> Amazon buys wholesale from you and resells. You lose pricing control but gain access to AMS (Amazon Marketing Services) and potentially better placement.</li>
          <li><strong>Recommendation:</strong> Stay 3P at this stage. 1P means Amazon controls your pricing, which erodes the premium positioning. Most beauty device brands at this scale prefer 3P.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

## Amazon Risk Factors
<!-- id: amazon-risk -->

<h3>What Could Go Wrong</h3>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Platform Risk</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Account suspension:</strong> Even large sellers get suspended for compliance issues. One wrong listing claim about "FDA-cleared" (vs. "FDA-registered") could trigger a review. Ensure all health claims are legally compliant.</li>
          <li><strong>Fee increases:</strong> Amazon raises fees annually. The 2024 inbound placement fee was a ~$0.30/unit surprise for many sellers. Budget for 5–10% annual fee increases.</li>
          <li><strong>Amazon Basics competition:</strong> Amazon has historically launched private-label products in dominant categories. Hair growth devices are niche enough that this is low-risk, but worth monitoring.</li>
          <li><strong>Review manipulation crackdown:</strong> Amazon is increasingly aggressive about detecting fake/incentivized reviews. iRESTORE's 29K organic reviews are a moat — protect them by ensuring all review generation is compliant.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Channel Conflict</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) enforcement:</strong> If Amazon price drops below MAP (via coupons or promotions), it undercuts DTC and wholesale. Strict MAP enforcement across all channels is critical.</li>
          <li><strong>Unauthorized sellers:</strong> Resellers buying from Costco and listing on Amazon at lower prices. Brand Registry + IP protection helps but doesn't eliminate this.</li>
          <li><strong>DTC cannibalization:</strong> Customers who would have bought on DTC (higher margin) buy on Amazon instead (lower margin). Smart pricing, bundling, and DTC-exclusive products help manage this.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="tip"><strong>Key consideration:</strong> MAP enforcement across channels is critical to prevent channel conflict. Without it, resellers can undercut pricing and erode brand value across all channels.</div>
