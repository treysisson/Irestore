## Areas to Improve
<!-- id: debrief-gaps -->

<div class="card card-red">
<h3>🔴 Missed CX Team Opportunity — Kevin Was Inviting a Number</h3>
Kevin said: <em>"I'm sure if you were to come on board, you would say, why the hell you have 25 people on this team."</em> You responded <em>"I was wondering"</em> — but didn't say WHAT you were wondering or propose a target. <strong>This was your COO wheelhouse.</strong> Kevin was explicitly inviting you to name a number or a structure.

<p class="muted"><strong>What to say next time:</strong> "Based on what you've described — AI + negotiation scripts dropping returns from 17% to 10% — I'd probably target 8-12 people plus a bot layer. But I'd want to audit the call transcripts first and give you specifics." Show initiative + specificity while acknowledging you need data.</p>
</div>

<div class="card card-red">
<h3>🔴 Didn't Probe Brandon's Current Setup</h3>
Kevin revealed Daniel was a "messenger" and that Brandon now reports directly to Kevin, <em>"which is way smoother."</em> But you didn't ask: "What does Brandon own end-to-end? Where do you still need to be involved?" This matters because <strong>you will inherit this team</strong> and need to know what's broken in ops right now.

<p class="muted"><strong>Fix for onsite:</strong> Ask Brandon directly about his ownership, pain points, and what he needs from a COO.</p>
</div>

<div class="card card-red">
<h3>🔴 Too Much Asking, Not Enough Proposing</h3>
Kevin flagged several fires (CX bloat, no AI on call transcripts, return rate still above target, email branding). You acknowledged each one but didn't propose solutions. Kevin is <strong>terrified of hiring another middleman</strong>. He explicitly said Daniel was a "messenger" and he "really hated it." He wants a COO who OWNS problems.

<p class="muted"><strong>Fix for onsite:</strong> Come with 2-3 specific proposals. Not full plans — just "Here's what I'd do in Week 1" level specifics. Show autonomy.</p>
</div>

<div class="card card-red">
<h3>🔴 Return Rate Analysis Was Surface-Level</h3>
Kevin revealed all phone calls are recorded and transcribed, plus emails are logged, but <em>"no one is doing mass AI transcript analysis."</em> You didn't propose doing it. At iRESTORE's scale, sentiment analysis on return calls would identify which scripts work, which agents retain best, and which objection categories are solvable.

<p class="muted"><strong>Fix for onsite:</strong> If given CX data in the exercise, run this analysis. Even a simple categorization of return reasons → retention rate by reason would be impressive.</p>
</div>
