## STRATEGIC QUESTIONS TO ASK KEVIN

### 1. **On Channel Prioritization** (Your Biggest Question)

**Ask:** "When you mention 'get into Sephora/Ulta for skincare,' is that your year-1 priority, or are we focused on scaling Costco and Amazon further first?"

**Why This Matters:**
- If Sephora/Ulta is priority → COO needs broker/rep team, retail packaging, demo budgets
- If Costco/Amazon is priority → COO needs supply chain and operational excellence
- These are different skill sets and capital requirements

**If Kevin says "Sephora/Ulta first":**
- "Unit economics show $15 profit per device at Sephora alone. That's brand investment, not profit. Are we comfortable with that?"
- "Have you thought about the bundle strategy—device + serum? That's where the margin math works."

**If Kevin says "Costco/Amazon first":**
- "Good. Costco's 15% of revenue is strategic gold. How deep should we go with SMU variants?"
- "What's the goal—maintain 15%, or grow it to 20%?"

---

### 2. **On Exit Narrative & Multi-Channel**

**Ask:** "How does multi-channel diversification fit into your 2-4 year exit timeline and $500M exit goal?"

**Why This Matters:**
- Buyers pay premiums for reduced concentration risk
- Pure Amazon play = risky (Amazon could change terms, de-list, reduce margins)
- Multi-channel (Costco + Retail + DTC) = lower risk = higher multiple

**Kevin Likely Says:** "I want to show we're not dependent on Amazon."

**Your Response:**
- "Understood. Multi-channel actually de-risks the business. Amazon at 50% of revenue is your concentration risk. If we hit 40-45% Amazon + 30% Costco + 20% Retail, you've got a much more valuable exit."
- "But this requires 18-24 months to execute. Are we aligned on timeline vs. exit target?"

### 3. **On Team & Hiring**

**Ask:** "Who currently owns retail relationships at Walmart, Target, Sephora, Ulta? Are they equipped to scale, or is this a new hire?"

**Why This Matters:**

- Need EDI/operations chops
- Need to know what's already in place vs. what you're building

**If Kevin Says:** "Brandon handles ops, but he's swamped."

**Your Response:**
- "Brandon sounds like an operations guy (execution, efficiency). Retail needs a dedicated head—someone who speaks broker language, has Sephora/Target contacts, knows co-op budgets. That's likely a new hire."

**Kevin Likely Says:** "I'm okay if it drives brand credibility and volume."

**Your Response:**

- "Also, bundles change the math. If we can sell device + serum as bundle at Sephora, margin gets to $50. That's viable."

## KEY METRICS TO RAISE

### If Kevin Asks: "What metrics matter most?"

**Your Answer:**
1. **Fill Rate (95%+)** — Ulta and Walmart chargebacks if you miss this. It's non-negotiable.
2. **Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)** — Retail requires faster turns than DTC. Track weekly sell-through.
3. **Gross Margin by Channel** — Watch for Sephora/Ulta not diluting blended margin below 50%.
4. **Amazon Concentration Risk** — Keep below 50% of revenue (currently 60-70%). This is your exit risk.
5. **Costco Revenue Growth** — It's your strategic lever. 15% → 20%? Track it monthly.

## WHAT NOT TO SAY

❌ "Sephora is going to be a game-changer for us."
- It's not. It's a brand play with weak unit economics. Walmart is the game-changer.

❌ "We need to get into omnichannel immediately."
- You're already omnichannel (DTC + Amazon + Costco). New channels are *addition*, not transformation.

❌ "Retail is lower margin, but that's okay because Amazon margin is so high."
- Shows you don't understand blended margin risk. The mix matters.

❌ "I'll hire a retail team to handle all this."
- Retail relationships need **you** in the room, not a team that reports to you.

❌ "MAP enforcement is complex, we'll figure it out later."
- Do it in week 1. If you don't, channel conflict will kill your margin before month 6.
