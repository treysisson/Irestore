## CHANNEL-SPECIFIC GOTCHAS

### Costco
- **Gotcha:** 12% margin is fixed. No negotiation. But volume justifies it.
- **Strategy:** Exclusive SMU (color/spec) prevents Amazon undercutting.
- **Success Factor:** "Stamp of trust" from Costco creates halo effect to other channels.

### Sephora
- **Gotcha:** Contract terms are "non-negotiable as presented" but persistent negotiation works. Use legal counsel.
- **Strategy:** Position sub-$200 wand as **gateway to skincare ecosystem** (consumables upside).
- **Success Factor:** Brand credibility + influencer seeding in Sephora Beauty Insider program.

### Ulta
- **Gotcha:** Stricter EDI than Sephora (30 days vs. flexible). Chargebacks for over-shipping.
- **Strategy:** Lower premium positioning than Sephora = better for accessibility (volume).
- **Success Factor:** 44M loyalty program = retargeting goldmine for repeat purchases.

### Walmart/Target
- **Gotcha:** 40% margin is tight. COGS discipline required. Anti-theft tagging adds cost.
- **Strategy:** Test with 500-store placement first, not national launch.
- **Success Factor:** In-store demo budget is critical. Target's beauty remodel program is friendlier than Walmart.

### Nordstrom
- **Gotcha:** Lower volume than Sephora/Ulta, but higher margin potential (40-50%). Premium positioning.
- **Strategy:** Reference CurrentBody success in Nordstrom. Position as premium alternative.
- **Success Factor:** Vendor partnership mindset. They want suppliers invested in sell-through.

### QVC/HSN
- **Gotcha:** 30-50% commission is huge. But one air slot = $50K-$500K in orders overnight.
- **Strategy:** Value segment (under $200 wand) better for live commerce than $600+ flagship.
- **Success Factor:** On-air personality + compelling demo. Beauty devices perform well (visual, results-driven).

### Amazon
- **Gotcha:** 8% referral fee is lower than retail, but high competitive pressure. Bundling is key.
- **Strategy:** Shift to bundle-only listings (device + serum/accessories) to prevent price comparison.
- **Success Factor:** Maintain 60-70% market share in category. Use Prime Day/Prime events.

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS BY ROLE

### For Kevin (CEO)
- **Focus:** Strategic channel mix (brand vs. volume). Decide Sephora/Ulta vs. Walmart/Target sequencing.
- **Decision:** Which channel do we enter first, and why?

### For COO
- **Focus:** Execution of 90-day retail readiness plan. Hiring retail operations team.
- **Decisions:**
  - EDI provider selection
  - Broker/rep firm selection
  - Packaging vendor lock-in
  - Retail fulfillment SOP

### For CFO
- **Focus:** Unit economics by channel. Co-op/MDF budgeting.
- **Decisions:**
  - Can we sustain 40%+ margins with packaging + co-op costs?
  - What's the payback timeline for Sephora/Ulta investment?
  - Cash flow impact of inventory position for retail?

### For VP Sales
- **Focus:** Buyer relationships, pitch materials, deal terms negotiation.
- **Decisions:**
  - Which buyer contacts to activate (RFP, warm intro)?
  - What incentive structure for broker/rep?
  - Deal terms negotiation (terms, net 30/60/90)?

## INTERVIEW QUESTIONS TO ASK KEVIN

1. **Strategic Intent:** "When you say 'get into Sephora/Ulta,' is that your priority in year 1, or are we focused on scaling Costco+Amazon further?"

2. **Channel Mix Vision:** "What's your target channel mix by year 3? (e.g., DTC 30%, Amazon 25%, Costco 20%, Retail 25%)"

3. **SMU Strategy:** "How far are we going with exclusive SKUs per channel? (Colors only, or different specs/features?)"

4. **Retail Operations:** "Who currently owns retail relationships (Walmart, Target, Amazon ops)? Are they ready to scale?"

5. **Packaging:** "Our current packaging works for DTC/Amazon. Are you prepared for a $25-50K redesign for retail?"

6. **Broker/Rep:** "Do we have a retail broker firm in mind, or is that a COO hire/responsibility?"

7. **Co-op Budget:** "What's your appetite for co-op/MDF spending in year 1 to launch new channels? ($300K? $500K?)"

8. **Exit Relevance:** "How does multi-channel diversification impact your 2-4 year exit timeline? (Reduces Amazon concentration = lower risk = higher multiple?)"
