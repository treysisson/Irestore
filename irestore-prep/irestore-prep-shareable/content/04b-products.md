<div class="tip">
<strong>💡 Key Observations for Interview:</strong><br>
1. <strong>Skincare is on Amazon but nascent</strong> — Face Mask has 610 reviews (4.6 stars), Neck & Chest has 94, Eye Mask has 72. Compare to Professional's 1,507 and Biotin Gummies' 4,329. Skincare Amazon is clearly a newer channel push — worth asking Kevin about the Amazon skincare growth trajectory and investment plans.<br>
2. <strong>Amazon catalog restructuring underway</strong> — legacy standalone device listings going "Out of Stock," replaced by higher-AOV bundles. This is smart but means temporarily losing #1 Best Seller badge momentum.<br>
3. <strong>AM/PM Duo at $19.99 (80% off $100)</strong> — this is almost certainly a loss leader to acquire skincare customers for the $299–$499 devices. Classic razor/blade play.<br>
4. <strong>Heavy discounting across consumables</strong> — 25–50% off most hair supplements. Could indicate excess inventory, price sensitivity testing, or aggressive customer acquisition strategy.<br>
5. <strong>The "31,638 reviews" claim is a marketing aggregate</strong> — combines ALL Amazon reviews (~12,850 verified across current listings) + Google Reviews (Shopping product reviews, Google Customer Reviews). The gap of ~18,800 likely includes Google Shopping reviews + older/delisted Amazon ASINs. Biotin Gummies alone account for 4,329 Amazon reviews (highest single product). This is standard DTC practice but worth understanding the composition.<br>
6. <strong>Eye Mask ($299) as gateway product</strong> — lowest-priced skincare device, marked "ALL-NEW," being promoted heavily. Could become the entry point for skincare the way Essential was for hair.
</div>
