## Most Likely Scenario: Blended Data Packet
<!-- id: live-ex-blended -->

<div class="card card-yellow">
<h3>📊 The "Here's Our Business — What Would You Do?" Exercise</h3>
<p>Kevin hands you a packet with P&L, channel data, CX metrics, org chart, and maybe inventory/ops data. You get an hour (maybe more). Come back with your top 3-5 recommendations.</p>

<p><strong>Why this is most likely:</strong> Kevin's whole concern is finding someone who can look at real business data, identify what matters, and come back with specific recommendations without hand-holding. That's the exact thing Daniel couldn't do. A blended packet tests breadth of thinking, prioritization, and whether you're an autonomous problem-solver or a "messenger."</p>

<p><strong>How to approach it:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Don't try to solve everything — pick the 3-5 highest-impact items</li>
<li>For each: state the problem, size the opportunity (even roughly), and propose a specific first step</li>
<li>Show you think in unit economics, not just "we should improve X"</li>
<li>Include at least one quick win (shows execution bias) and one strategic bet (shows vision)</li>
</ul>
</div>

<p><strong>Step 2: Where did the 8 points of margin go?</strong> Walk down the red-highlighted cells — anything that grew faster than revenue (i.e., % of rev went UP):</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Amazon fees (+1.6pp):</strong> Biggest single leak. Amazon mix growing and/or FBA rates increasing. $1.4M drag at $90M. Ask about SFP for oversized SKUs.</li>
<li><strong>Amazon PPC (+1.4pp):</strong> Getting more expensive to acquire on Amazon. Competition heating up (Capillus, HairMax). Negative keyword audit is a quick win.</li>
<li><strong>Meta/Google (+1.2pp):</strong> DTC CAC rising. Creative fatigue? New TikTok spend unoptimized? Ask for blended CAC by channel.</li>

<li><strong>Freight & Duties (+1pp):</strong> Volume growth without rate renegotiation. Low-hanging fruit — competitive bid the Topocean contract.</li>
<li><strong>Costco (+0.8pp):</strong> Costco growing but co-op costs and 30% lifetime returns are brutal. Need a fully-loaded channel P&L.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Step 3: Scan the green cells — what's working?</strong> Don't only be negative — call out wins:</p>

<p><strong>What to say:</strong> "Every percentage point of mix shift from DTC to Costco has a working capital cost. At 20% Costco, you're carrying $4M in receivables that wouldn't exist at DTC. That's the hidden cost of retail channel expansion."</p>

## General Prep: How to Approach Any Exercise
<!-- id: live-ex-general -->

<div class="card card-green">
<h3>✅ The Framework That Works for Kevin</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Start with "what jumps out"</strong> — show pattern recognition and prioritization, not exhaustive analysis</li>
<li><strong>Size the opportunity</strong> — Kevin thinks in dollars, not abstractions. "This is costing you $X" or "This could save $Y"</li>
<li><strong>Propose a specific first step</strong> — not "we should look into this" but "in Week 1, I would do X"</li>
<li><strong>Show cross-functional thinking</strong> — connect ops to finance to CX to marketing. Kevin's frustration with Brandon is siloed thinking</li>
<li><strong>Be honest about what you don't know</strong> — "I'd need to see X before making this call" shows judgment, not weakness</li>
</ol>
</div>

<div class="card card-red">
<h3>🚫 What NOT to Do</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Don't be a messenger:</strong> "Here's what I found, what do you think?" is the Daniel anti-pattern. Kevin wants "Here's what I found, here's what I'd do, here's when it'll be done."</li>
<li><strong>Don't over-analyze:</strong> Kevin said "exercise of prompts" — he wants to see how you think, not a consulting deck</li>
<li><strong>Don't propose without specifics:</strong> "We should optimize CX" is worthless. "Cut to 12 people, add a bot layer, save $400K/year" is what he wants to hear</li>
<li><strong>Don't ignore the people side:</strong> Every operational change has a team impact. Acknowledge it. Kevin is learning to fire people — he needs a COO who handles those conversations, not avoids them</li>
</ul>
</div>
