<h3>🎙️ Business Rock Stars (~2021)</h3>

<p class="muted">Host: Brittany Whitney | <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=onLhzbvPTUo" target="_blank">Watch on YouTube →</a></p>

<div class="card">
  <div class="card-header">Key Takeaways for Your Interview</div>
  <table>
    <tr><th>Topic</th><th>Insight</th><th>Your Angle</th></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Origin story</strong></td><td>Kevin's father ran Freedom Laser Therapy (smoking cessation). When he experienced hair loss, they pivoted to home-use laser hair therapy. Kevin joined right out of college — "didn't know what he wanted to do."</td><td>Kevin values the long game. He spent 3 years on R&D before selling a single unit. Reference this patience when discussing infrastructure investment.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>FDA clearance as moat</strong></td><td>The FDA 510(k) process took ~3 years with multiple rounds of back-and-forth. Kevin sees this as a deliberate competitive advantage: "sometimes it's worth it to do the really difficult things because it creates that edge."</td><td>Show you understand moat-building through operational rigor — not just marketing.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Team at ~40 people</strong></td><td>At time of recording, team was ~40. Kevin was still hands-on in most areas. His biggest challenge: "hiring is the biggest challenge" and wrong hires cost "so much time and money."</td><td>Demonstrate your hiring playbook — structured process, culture-fit screening, fast performance evaluation.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>COVID pivot</strong></td><td>Moved to hybrid remote (Slack, Zoom, Loom). E-commerce benefited from lockdowns. Proved remote work could work for the company.</td><td>You have experience managing distributed teams through crises. This resonates with Kevin's experience.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>5-year vision</strong></td><td>"We want to be much more than a hair growth device company... your one-stop shop for all things beauty." Targets: Sephora, Target, Costco, international expansion, anti-aging.</td><td>This vision requires operational infrastructure you can build — retail ops, international supply chain, multi-category inventory management.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Delegation struggle</strong></td><td>"I felt like I had to do everything myself... which is absolutely not true. You need to hire people, delegate, and fire people who don't work out."</td><td>This is exactly why he's hiring a COO. Position yourself as the person who lets him stop doing everything.</td></tr>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="answer-block">
  <strong>Notable quote:</strong> "Your highest leverage thing no longer becomes [driving sales] and it becomes people — becomes managing people, becomes leading people, and more importantly hiring the best people you can find out there for the job." <span class="muted">— Kevin, Business Rock Stars</span>
</div>

<p class="muted">Host: Eric Dick | <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Ej1UMdUGtI" target="_blank">Watch on YouTube →</a></p>

<div class="card">

    <tr><th>Topic</th><th>Insight</th><th>Your Angle</th></tr>

This is their primary paid acquisition engine.</td><td>Shows sophistication in marketing ops. Your role: ensure the ops infrastructure (attribution, analytics, content pipeline) supports this engine at scale.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>TikTok restrictions</strong></td><td>Medical device advertising restricted on TikTok. Limits a major growth channel. Short-form video (Shorts, Reels) experimental with cheap CPCs but poor conversion.</td><td>Regulatory constraints create ops complexity. You can help navigate this with compliant content strategies and channel diversification.</td></tr>

Also used brandmark.io for AI logo generation.</td><td>Shows Kevin is open to AI tools. If you have experience implementing AI-powered customer experiences, this is a strong resonance point.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>$50K marketing budget allocation</strong></td><td>If given $50K: Shopify theme ($5-10K), landing pages, 3D product animations ($10K), rest in influencers/UGC/ads.</td><td>Kevin is capital-efficient. He respects scrappy resourcefulness. Frame your operational improvements as high-ROI, low-cost investments.</td></tr>
  </table>
</div>

<h3>🎙️ Money Talks (~2025, Most Recent)</h3>

<p class="muted">Host: Jesse | <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vp0qQw4UiQc" target="_blank">Watch on YouTube →</a></p>

<div class="tip"><strong>This is the most important podcast for interview prep.</strong> It's the most recent (~2025) and reveals Kevin's current thinking on hiring, scaling, and exit.</div>

<div class="card">
  <div class="card-header">Key Takeaways for Your Interview</div>
  <table>
    <tr><th>Topic</th><th>Insight</th><th>Your Angle</th></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Company scale</strong></td><td>~80 employees. Mid-eight figures revenue growing beyond. "Hundreds of millions" in cumulative product sales. Products $500–$2,000 range.</td><td>This is the scale where operational infrastructure becomes the bottleneck. Process, systems, and people management are the levers — exactly your wheelhouse.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>ABCD trust framework</strong></td><td><strong>A</strong>uthentic reviews, <strong>B</strong>efore/afters, <strong>C</strong>linical studies, <strong>D</strong>octors/dermatologists. 43% average hair improvement in 4-month double-blind study.</td><td>This framework is the operational backbone of their marketing. Ensure each element has robust systems behind it — review collection, photo verification, study management, doctor partnerships.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Face mask launch</strong></td><td>Launched ~6 months ago. Originated from customer surveys — 25% of open-field responses asked for LED face mask. Kevin: "The customers told us exactly what they wanted."</td><td>Shows Kevin's data-driven product development. Reference this as an example of the kind of customer feedback loop you'd systematize at scale.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Head of growth = game changer</strong></td><td>Hiring a head of growth marketing was Kevin's single most impactful hire. It freed him to think strategically instead of managing campaigns daily.</td><td>This is exactly the outcome Kevin wants from the COO hire — but for the entire operations side of the business. Mirror this story: "Just like your head of growth freed you from marketing, I'll free you from operations."</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Value creation vs. value protection</strong></td><td>Kevin categorizes roles into two buckets. "Hold on to value-driving things as long as you can. Delegate value protection first."</td><td>Position yourself as someone who does both — protects value through systems AND creates value through margin improvement, international expansion, and exit readiness.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>A/B/C player framework</strong></td><td>"B players are the worst — they're not bad enough to fire but they're an opportunity cost to the A players." Recently exited "many directors and up."</td><td>You're an A player who identifies and addresses B-player problems quickly. Share a specific example of upgrading a team by making tough calls.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Business coach (P&G background)</strong></td><td>Coach taught Kevin channel pricing strategy (Costco SMU/special skew) that "saved six figures." Kevin believes in paying for expertise.</td><td>Kevin already values paying a premium for been-there-done-that experience. Your salary ask isn't a cost — it's a $1,000/hr investment in someone who's navigated exactly this growth phase before.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>$1,000/hr framework</strong></td><td>Kevin calculates his effective hourly rate (EBITDA ÷ hours worked) and uses it as a filter: "If it's below $1,000/hr, I need someone else to do it."</td><td>Frame your impact in these terms: "If my operational improvements drive 3 margin points on $120M, that's $3.6M in annual EBITDA. At a 2,000-hour year, that's $1,800/hr of value I'm creating."</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Thinking time framework</strong></td><td>Kevin blocks Fridays as creative/thinking day — no meetings. Uses structured prompts for strategic thinking sessions.</td><td>Show you respect deep work. Offer to protect Kevin's thinking time by being the operational firewall that handles the day-to-day without needing his input.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Critical early DTC decision</strong></td><td>Early on, turned down a mid-5-figure wholesale distributor to stay DTC. Competitor who went Groupon/wholesale route is no longer around.</td><td>Kevin values strategic discipline — saying no to easy revenue that dilutes brand value. Show you have the same long-term thinking.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Exit considerations</strong></td><td>"Most of my value is tied up in equity." Inventory-heavy, reinvesting everything. Not ruling out exit but focused on growth. Mobile app in development.</td><td>You're the person who prepares the business for exit — clean financials, documented processes, scalable systems, strong management team. This is your core value proposition.</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>"Two rungs up the ladder"</strong></td><td>Kevin references Alex Hormozi's concept: network with people 2 levels ahead, not 10. More actionable advice, relevant context.</td><td>You ARE Kevin's "two rungs up" — someone who's already run operations at the scale he's growing into. Frame yourself this way.</td></tr>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="answer-block">
  <strong>Notable quote:</strong> "At a certain scale, you really have to hire the people that have been there, done that, and are better than you so you can truly let go. I've brought on people where I felt like I was better than them at their job. And what that meant was I was always hovering over their shoulders... It's not empowering." <span class="muted">— Kevin, Money Talks</span>
</div>

<div class="answer-block">
  <strong>On role leverage:</strong> "It comes down to whether that role has enough leverage to become 10x. The best warehouse worker is maybe 50% faster than an average warehouse worker. But a growth marketer — especially today with AI — can probably 20x the next person. So I want to make sure to think through how much leverage the role has." <span class="muted">— Kevin, Money Talks</span>
</div>
