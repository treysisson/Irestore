## Priority Questions (In Order)
<!-- id: q-priority -->

<p class="muted">These are the 9 questions you plan to ask, in this order. Each is framed to show strategic thinking while surfacing information you actually need.</p>

<ol>
        <li><strong>"Can you help me understand the ops leadership structure? I saw reference to an SVP of Ops — is that Brandon, or is that a separate role? Is that person still with the company?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: You need to understand the org chart you're inheriting. If an SVP Ops left recently, you want to know why — was it a performance issue, culture misfit, or scope change? This also clarifies Brandon's actual role and whether you'd be backfilling, restructuring, or building from scratch. Listen for: tone when discussing the departure, whether Kevin takes responsibility or externalizes it.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"You mentioned Japan and Korea as target markets. Have you started any regulatory groundwork — engaged MFDS or PMDA consultants, or mapped out the approval pathways?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: You already know from your first conversation that Kevin said Japan is "quite stringent" and they're "looking into it." This question shows you know the specific agencies (MFDS for Korea, PMDA for Japan) and are ready to drive the process. If they haven't started, it's a clear Day 1 project. If they have, you need to know what stage. Korea is the faster path (6–12 months for MFDS class II); Japan PMDA can take 12–24 months.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"What's the current channel mix — DTC, Amazon, Costco/wholesale — and how do you see that evolving over the next 1–2 years? And separately, how are you thinking about international vs. domestic as a growth axis?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: Channel mix and geographic expansion are different strategic dimensions, but both shape how you allocate resources and build infrastructure. Amazon likely dominates revenue but at lower margins; DTC has better unit economics but higher CAC. Understanding Kevin's vision for the mix tells you where to focus operational investment. B2B/medspas could be a high-margin channel with different fulfillment needs. Listen for: whether he has specific targets or it's more aspirational.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"What's the device return rate running at? When units come back, are you able to refurbish and resell, or are returns a full write-off? I'd love to understand the top return reasons and what you've tried so far to bring the rate down."</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: The job scorecard targets &lt;8% returns, which implies the current rate is north of 10%. At $695–$1,395 per device, each percentage point of returns is ~$1.4M. This question shows you understand reverse logistics and its P&L impact. Listen for: whether they track return reasons rigorously, whether they have a "save the sale" process, and whether Costco's generous return policy is inflating the blended number. Refurbishment capability is a significant margin lever — if they're destroying returned units, that's a quick win.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"What's been the tariff impact on your import costs? Have you explored refund claims after the Supreme Court's IEEPA ruling in February?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: On Feb 20, 2026, SCOTUS ruled 6–3 in <em>Learning Resources v. Trump</em> that IEEPA tariffs were unconstitutional. Importers may be eligible for refunds — estimated $175–$179B industry-wide. If iRESTORE imports from Shenzhen (they do), they likely paid significant IEEPA tariffs since 2025. Refund claims have filing deadlines — this is time-sensitive and could be a material cash recovery. Even raising this shows you're on top of current trade policy. Sources: <a href="https://www.scotusblog.com/2026/02/supreme-court-strikes-down-tariffs/" target="_blank">SCOTUSblog</a>, <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/20/supreme-court-trump-tariff-decision-illegal-refunds.html" target="_blank">CNBC</a></span></li>

        <li><strong>"I want to talk about the working dynamic. What's the ideal cadence with a COO — daily standups, weekly 1:1s, async? And when we disagree on a direction — because we will — how do you want me to handle that?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: Kevin has said he wants a "partner and mentor, not just a report." This question takes that at face value and gets specific. The disagreement question is critical — it reveals whether Kevin truly wants pushback or just wants execution. His answer tells you how much autonomy you'll actually have. Also listen for: does he want you in the weeds with him or running the show independently? The answer shapes your first 90 days.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"I've been looking at LYMA — they're doing $48M with cold laser devices at $2,700–$6,000 price points, same LLLT technology platform you use for hair. Is cold laser on the skincare roadmap, or are you sticking with LED for now?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: This shows you're thinking about product strategy, not just operations. LYMA's cold laser is 100x more powerful than LED panels and commands 2–4x iRESTORE's skincare pricing. iRESTORE already has LLLT expertise from hair devices — cold laser skincare could be a natural high-ASP extension. Kevin's answer reveals how ambitious the product roadmap is and whether the COO will have input on product strategy. If he's not aware of LYMA, you've just added value.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"Are you planning clinical trials for the beauty/skincare devices, or relying on the existing 510(k) pathway with predicate devices? Your hair device trial showing 43% growth in 16 weeks is a real differentiator — is the plan to replicate that for skincare?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: iRESTORE ran their own <a href="https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03331003" target="_blank">clinical trial (NCT03331003)</a> for hair devices, which most competitors didn't do (they rely on predicate device data). If iRESTORE does the same for skincare/body, it's a massive competitive moat. If they're not planning to, that's a strategic conversation worth having. This also shows deep product knowledge that goes beyond surface-level research.</span></li>

        <li><strong>"Help me understand the CX team — I see roughly 50 agents in the Philippines, but also job postings in South America, Europe, and elsewhere. What's driving the geographic expansion? And how much AI have you incorporated into the support workflow so far?"</strong>
        <br><span class="muted">Why this matters: A 50-person offshore CX team at $90M revenue suggests a high-touch model — which makes sense for $700–$1,400 devices where pre-sale education and post-sale support drive satisfaction and reduce returns. But if they're hiring globally, either they're chasing timezone coverage, cost-arbitraging against Philippines wages, or the team is growing faster than revenue justifies. The AI question is the real one: at this scale, AI-assisted CX (auto-triage, suggested responses, FAQ deflection, sentiment routing) can dramatically improve resolution time and reduce headcount needs. If they haven't started, that's a Day 1 initiative. If they have, you want to know what's working and what's not. This also lets you signal your AI fluency and connects to the "grow capacity without growing headcount proportionally" point from your mock answers.</span></li>
      </ol>

## Question Bank
<!-- id: q-bank -->

<p class="muted">Additional questions to consider — move any of these into your priority list as the conversation evolves.</p>

<h3>Strategic & Vision</h3>
<ul>
        <li>"What does the $300–500M revenue version of iRESTORE look like to you? How much of that is hair vs. skincare vs. B2B vs. international?"</li>
        <li>"You mentioned Therabody as an aspirational comp. What specifically about their trajectory resonates — the product expansion, the retail distribution, or the category creation?"</li>
        <li>"When you think about the exit in 2–4 years, are you leaning more toward strategic (L'Oréal, Shiseido type) or financial (PE) buyer? That shapes how we build."</li>
        <li>"What's the one thing that keeps you up at night about the business right now?"</li>
</ul>

<h3>Operations & Execution</h3>
<ul>
        <li>"Walk me through a typical product development cycle — from concept to shelf. Where are the bottlenecks?"</li>
        <li>"What does your inventory planning process look like today? Who owns it?"</li>
        <li>"How do you think about customer lifetime value? Do you have repeat purchase data? What does the post-purchase journey look like?"</li>
        <li>"What's the biggest operational fire you've had to put out in the last 6 months besides Q4?"</li>
        <li>"What's the current split between FBA and FBM on Amazon? And what's the margin difference between DTC, Amazon, and any wholesale channels like Costco?"</li>
</ul>

<h3>Leadership & Relationship</h3>
<ul>
        <li>"What happened with the people who didn't work out in senior roles before? What went wrong?"</li>
        <li>"What does success look like for this role at the 6-month mark vs. the 12-month mark? What would make you say 'this was the right hire'?"</li>
        <li>"You've talked about the evolution from doing to managing — what specifically would you want to hand off to a COO in the first 30 days?"</li>
        <li>"What did you learn from the recent leadership transitions that should inform how we build the team going forward?"</li>
</ul>

<h3>Deep Research / Show Your Homework</h3>
<ul>
        <li>"You've talked about moving from being a device company to a beauty brand — how far along is that rebrand, and what's the next phase?"</li>
        <li>"The Costco partnership was a massive lever. What other retail or distribution opportunities are you exploring?"</li>
        <li>"How do you think about the body panel category? Is the goal to dominate like you did with hair devices?"</li>
        <li>"Your clinical trial data showing 43% hair growth in 16 weeks is a real differentiator. How are you leveraging that in marketing vs. competitors who only have FDA clearance without their own clinical studies?"</li>
</ul>
