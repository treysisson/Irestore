## Lever: Returns (10% → <8%)
<!-- id: pnl-returns -->

<h3>Returns & Allowances — estimated ~10% of gross / $10.5M</h3>
<div class="tip"><strong>Key insight:</strong> The job scorecard sets a target of "Returns &lt;8%," which implies the current rate is well above 8% — likely 10–12%. At ~$105M gross, each 1pp of return reduction is worth <strong>$1.1M</strong> straight to the bottom line. Hitting the scorecard target of &lt;8% alone would save <strong>$2.1–4.2M</strong>.</div>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Improve unboxing & setup experience:</strong> Most device returns come from confusion or unmet expectations. Create video QR codes in packaging for first-use walkthrough.</li>
  <li><strong>"Save the Sale" retention team:</strong> Before processing a return, route to a specialist who troubleshoots. Many device returns are "I don't know if it's working" — addressable with education.</li>
  <li><strong>Pre-purchase expectation setting:</strong> Add "Results timeline" content (visible in 3–6 months) to listings and DTC. Mismatched expectations are a top return driver.</li>
  <li><strong>Return reason analysis:</strong> Categorize every return by reason code. "Didn't work" vs. "didn't see results" vs. "wrong product" vs. "defective" each require different interventions.</li>
  <li><strong>Channel-specific return rates:</strong> Amazon vs. DTC vs. Costco return rates are likely very different. Costco's generous return policy could be inflating the blended number.</li>
</ul>

## Lever: COGS (33% → 30%)
<!-- id: pnl-cogs -->

<h3>Product Manufacturing — currently ~25% of net rev / $22.5M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Volume-based renegotiation:</strong> At $90M+, even 2–3% improvement on component pricing = $450K–675K. Annual RFQ process with 2–3 alternative suppliers keeps pricing honest.</li>
  <li><strong>Value engineering:</strong> Work with Longchuan Huang's PD team to identify components that can be simplified without affecting efficacy. LED driver boards, housing materials, connectors are common targets.</li>
  <li><strong>SKU rationalization:</strong> Fewer SKUs = longer production runs = lower per-unit cost. If bottom 20% of SKUs drive &lt;5% of revenue, consider sunsetting.</li>
  <li><strong>Family manufacturing advantage:</strong> Kevin's father's Shenzhen facility + net-150 payment terms = significant cash flow and cost advantage vs. competitors. Protect this relationship while professionalizing procurement.</li>
</ul>

A 33% CM means every dollar of revenue generates $0.33 to cover fixed costs and profit.</div>

## Lever: Amazon Fees (11.5%)
<!-- id: pnl-amazon -->

<h3>Amazon Fees — currently ~11.5% / $10.4M (referral $4.5M + FBA $5.9M)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Shift volume to DTC:</strong> Every $1M moved from Amazon to DTC saves ~$230K in combined referral + FBA fees, minus the cost of DTC fulfillment and payment processing (~$130K). Net ~$100K improvement per $1M shifted, before marketing costs.</li>
  <li><strong>FBA inventory optimization:</strong> Just-in-time replenishment to avoid long-term storage fees. Keep IPI score high.</li>
  <li><strong>Seller Fulfilled Prime:</strong> For oversized or slow-moving SKUs, SFP avoids FBA fees while keeping Prime badge.</li>
  <li><strong>Amazon 60–70% of category:</strong> With dominant market share, iRESTORE has negotiating leverage for co-op advertising credits and promotional placement.</li>
</ul>

## Lever: People (9%)
<!-- id: pnl-people -->

<h3>People — currently ~9% / $8.1M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Headcount:</strong> ~80 FTE + 50 overseas + contractors = 100+ total. ~$81K avg loaded cost per FTE equivalent.</li>
  <li><strong>Nearshore Tier 1 CS:</strong> Move basic CS (order status, simple troubleshooting) to nearshore team (Mexico, Colombia). Keep Tier 2 onshore. Typical savings: 40–60% on CS labor.</li>
  <li><strong>Performance-based comp:</strong> Align bonuses to contribution margin, not just revenue, to prevent growth-at-any-cost behavior.</li>
  <li><strong>EOS structure:</strong> ~60% adopted per Kevin. Full rollout creates accountability without adding headcount.</li>
</ul>
