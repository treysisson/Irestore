## 1. COSTCO

### Margin Requirements
**VERIFIED:** Costco takes **12% gross margin** (matches Kevin's claim exactly)

**Context:** Costco operates on the thinnest margins in retail (11.4-14% overall), which they pass directly to members as value. This is their competitive moat—not negotiable.

### Key Vendor Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **EDI Compliance** | Mandatory before products reach warehouse floor. Must comply with Costco's exact specifications for orders, ASNs (Advance Ship Notices), invoices, and shipping labels. |
| **Volume Capacity** | Costco ships higher volumes per shipment vs. other retailers. Suppliers must have inventory and production capacity to handle bulk orders. |
| **Slotting Fees** | Costco typically **does NOT charge slotting fees** (unlike traditional retail). They absorb these costs as part of their member-focused model. |
| **Demonstration Costs** | Vendors may pay for in-store sampling/demos. Costco covers setup, staff, materials—you pay the cost. |

### Revenue Impact
- **iRESTORE Reality:** Costco went from **0% to 15% of revenue** in 2022 (Costco.com launch). Kevin called this "a few emails and a few phone calls."
- **Success Factor:** Once Costco carries a product, the "Costco stamp" builds trust that you can't buy elsewhere. This brand halo extends to other channels.

### Strategic Advantage
- Lowest wholesale margin (12%) actually creates a **moat**: competitors can't compete on price, so Costco exclusivity drives perception of quality.
- iRESTORE runs **Costco-exclusive products or colors** (SMU strategy) to prevent price undercutting on Amazon or DTC.

## DEFINITIONS & STRATEGIC CONCEPTS

### SMU (Special Make-Up) / Special SKU
**Definition (from Kevin's podcast):** "Special skew basically for that dedicated channel. Maybe it's different color, maybe it's different whatever but inherently a different product and so you're not in price competition."

**Formal Definition:** SMU = custom/exclusive product made specifically for one retailer. Different from standard SKU (different color, packaging, or specifications).

**Strategic Value:**
- Eliminates **channel conflict** on pricing (Costco can't be undercut by Amazon if the Costco version is exclusive)
- Retailers prefer SMUs (they get unique inventory, can't be price-compared)
- Manufacturing complexity: must track separate COGS for each SMU

**iRESTORE Example:** Costco carries exclusive colors/variants of devices that aren't sold on Amazon or DTC. This allows 12% Costco margin without price parity conflict.

**Implementation Cost:** Higher: requires separate SKU management, often higher MOQs per SKU.

## MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) POLICY

### What is MAP?
**Definition:** Lowest price a reseller can **advertise/display publicly**. Does NOT regulate actual sale price (in-cart/checkout pricing can be lower).

### Why Beauty Brands Use MAP
- Maintains premium brand perception
- Prevents race-to-bottom discounting
- Protects retailer margins
- Encourages retailers to stock/promote the product

### Multi-Channel MAP Enforcement
**Internet MAP (iMAP):** Separate minimum prices for online channels to account for Amazon, marketplace, and DTC dynamics.

**Enforcement Challenge:** Requires automated monitoring across:
- Amazon
- Retailer websites (Sephora.com, Ulta.com, Costco.com, Target.com)
- Marketplace sellers (3P on Amazon)
- DTC website (irestorelaser.com)

**Violation Penalties:** Supply restrictions, account suspension, chargebacks.

### iRESTORE Strategy
Job description mentions: **"MAP policy finalized (wk 2)"** as part of 90-day retail readiness plan. This is critical before onboarding retail:
- $199 MSRP for wand at retail
- DTC pricing (irestorelaser.com) must respect MAP
- Amazon pricing must be monitored
- If retail enters and undercuts, enforcement mechanisms activate

## RETAILER REQUIREMENTS: EDI, PACKAGING, FILL RATE, INSURANCE

### EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) Requirements

| Retailer | EDI Timeline | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **Ulta** | 30 days | 850 (PO), 856 (ASN), 810 (Invoice) |
| **Nordstrom** | 60 days | 850, 856, 810 + UPC compatibility test |
| **Costco** | At launch | Costco-specific specs for orders, ASNs, labels |
| **Walmart/Target** | At launch | EDIFACT/ANSI X12 standards |
| **Sephora** | Not explicitly required | May use portal-based order system |

**Cost:** $500-$2000/month for third-party EDI provider (SPS Commerce, OpenText).

### Packaging Requirements

| Category | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Barcodes** | GS1-certified UPC on product and case level |
| **Labeling** | Specific placement per retailer (top-left carton, spine, etc.) |
| **Case Packs** | 12-pack, 24-pack, 48-pack (varies by retailer) |
| **Retail-Ready Packaging (RRP)** | Shelf-ready displays (Walmart/Target), hanger holes, perforated opens |
| **Anti-Theft** | For items >$150, source tagging via Sensormatic (1-800-642-7505) |
| **Safety Labels** | SDS sheets for hazardous materials (Nordstrom), regulatory warnings (FDA for devices) |

**Cost Impact:** Redesign retail-specific packaging = $10K-$50K setup + 15-20% higher packaging cost per unit.

### Fill Rate Standards
**Definition:** % of units successfully delivered vs. ordered on time.

**Consequences of Low Fill Rate:**
- Chargebacks (retailer withholds payment for shortage)
- Reduced future POs
- Account suspension (if <85% fill rate)

**Typical Retail Expectation:** 95%+ fill rate within 2 business days of order.

### Insurance Minimums

| Insurer Requirement | Minimum Limit |
|---|---|
| **Commercial General Liability** | $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate |
| **Product Liability** | $1,000,000 per occurrence (higher for electronics/devices) |
| **Auto Liability** | $1,000,000 (if doing your own logistics) |

**Certificate of Insurance (COI):**
- Required before shipping to retailer
- Amazon: Required at $10K/month sales threshold
- Walmart Marketplace: Required if >$100K GMV or on demand

**Cost:** $1,500-$3,500/year for adequate coverage (beauty device category).

## MULTI-CHANNEL PRICING & CHANNEL CONFLICT MANAGEMENT

### The Challenge
You operate across:
1. **DTC** (irestorelaser.com) — Full margin, brand control
2. **Amazon** — 8% referral + ~5% FBA, volume but competitive
3. **Costco** — 12% margin, huge volume, exclusive products
4. **Retail** (Sephora/Ulta/Target) — 40-50% margin, brand positioning
5. **QVC/HSN** — 30-50% commission, volume spike but lower repeat

### Pricing Parity Solutions

| Strategy | How It Works | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SMU (Exclusive SKUs)** | Different color/specs per channel | No price comparison possible | Higher COGS, inventory complexity |
| **MAP Enforcement** | Set advertised floor, monitor violations | Protects margins, prevents race-to-bottom | Enforcement costly, brand conflict risk |
| **Channel Segmentation** | DTC = premium, Retail = standard, Amazon = volume | Clear messaging | Complexity in messaging |
| **Dynamic Bundling** | Sell device as bundle (device + accessories) on each channel | Different product = different price | Complexity, lower ASP on DTC |

### iRESTORE's Approach
From interview prep materials:
- **DTC/Costco:** Core brand positioning, premium pricing
- **Amazon:** Volume play, competitive pricing, bundles
- **Sephora/Ulta:** New channel, positioning as gateway to ecosystem

**Execution:** MAP policy + SMU strategy (Costco exclusive colors, retail exclusive packaging).

### What Retailers Expect (Typical)

| Channel | Requirement | Cost to Vendor |
|---|---|---|
| **Costco** | In-warehouse sampling events (2-4 per year per location) | Staffing + materials (~$500-$1000/event) |

### MDF (Marketing Development Funds) Explained
- **Co-op Model:** Brand accrues funds based on % of sales (e.g., 10% of wholesale sales becomes MDF budget)
- **Uses:** Advertising, promotions, trade shows, product training, events
- **iRESTORE Context:** For Sephora/Ulta launch, budget MDF = ~10-15% of first-year volume to fund launch events, sampling, in-store demos
