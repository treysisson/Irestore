Entering South Korea first builds market data and regulatory precedent that strengthens the Japan application. Both require local representatives, full document translation, and device classification as medical devices.</p>

## South Korea – MFDS Pathway
<!-- id: reg-korea -->

<h2>South Korea: MFDS Registration for Hair Growth Devices</h2>

      <h3>Classification & Approval Body</h3>
      <p>Hair growth laser/LED devices are classified as <strong>medical devices</strong> (not cosmetics) in South Korea under the MFDS. LG's Pra.L MediHair — a near-identical product to iRESTORE — was approved as a <strong>Class II medical device</strong> after FDA clearance. This is a positive predicate for iRESTORE.</p>
      <p>Class II devices go through MDITAC/NIDS certification (faster than direct MFDS approval for Class III). This is the most likely path for iRESTORE.</p>

      <h3>Registration Process (6-Step Path)</h3>
      <table>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Description</th><th>Timeline</th><th>Cost (USD)</th></tr>
        <tr><td>1</td><td><strong>Appoint Korea License Holder (KLH)</strong> — mandatory local entity with import business license and quality manager</td><td>2–4 weeks</td><td>$2,000–$5,000/yr</td></tr>
        <tr><td>2</td><td><strong>K-GMP Certification</strong> — on-site manufacturing audit required BEFORE registration</td><td>1–3 months</td><td>$4,000–$6,000</td></tr>
        <tr><td>3</td><td><strong>Technical File (STED)</strong> — device specs, safety/performance data, biocompatibility, clinical data, all in Korean</td><td>6–8 weeks</td><td>$8,000–$15,000</td></tr>
        <tr><td>4</td><td><strong>Korean Translation</strong> — all documents, labeling, IFU</td><td>4–6 weeks</td><td>$2,000–$4,000</td></tr>
        <tr><td>5</td><td><strong>MDITAC Submission & Review</strong> — Class II certification pathway</td><td>2–6 months</td><td>$1,600 (official fee)</td></tr>
        <tr><td>6</td><td><strong>Post-Approval</strong> — labeling finalization, customs docs, initial shipment</td><td>1–2 months</td><td>$1,500–$3,000</td></tr>
        <tr class="highlight-row"><td></td><td><strong>TOTAL</strong></td><td><strong>9–18 months</strong></td><td><strong>$32,000–$76,000</strong></td></tr>
      </table>

      <h3>Testing Requirements</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Electrical Safety & EMC:</strong> IEC 60601-1 and IEC 60601-1-2 compliance</li>
        <li><strong>Laser Safety:</strong> KS C IEC 60825-1 (Korean adoption) — power output, wavelength, classification, warning labels</li>
        <li><strong>Biocompatibility:</strong> ISO 10993-1 — may be waived if predicate device data available from FDA filing</li>
        <li><strong>Thermal Safety:</strong> Temperature rise limits during extended use</li>
        <li><strong>Performance:</strong> Light output specifications, wavelength validation, durability cycling</li>
      </ul>
      <div class="tip"><strong>Cost Saver:</strong> iRESTORE's existing FDA 510(k) data — biocompatibility, electrical safety, laser safety testing — can be leveraged for the Korean submission. This could save $5,000–$20,000 in redundant testing.</div>

      <h3>Advertising Restrictions (Critical)</h3>
      <p>South Korea is <strong>extremely strict</strong> on hair loss product claims. The MFDS reported that 77.8% of illegal hair product enforcement cases were for claims like "promotes new hair growth." Permitted claims must be backed by clinical data and aligned with the MFDS registration.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Prohibited:</strong> "Promotes new hair growth," "increases hair thickness," "prevents hair loss," "cures alopecia"</li>
        <li><strong>Permitted (with data):</strong> "For use in treating androgenetic alopecia" (if approved as medical device), "stimulates hair follicles" (if mechanistically supported)</li>
        <li><strong>FDA-cleared:</strong> Can reference FDA clearance only if also MFDS-approved</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>E-Commerce Landscape</h3>
      <p><strong>Coupang</strong> (market leader) explicitly bans medical device direct sales through its standard marketplace. Strategy: partner with a licensed Korean medical device distributor who sells through Coupang. <strong>Amazon Korea</strong> and <strong>Gmarket</strong> have similar restrictions — all require MFDS registration and KLH compliance.</p>
      <div class="tip"><strong>Viable Strategy:</strong> Direct sales via company website (in Korean) + partnership with licensed Korean distributor for marketplace presence. B2C direct shipping from US with KLH as importer of record for initial testing.</div>

      <h3>IP Protection</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Patents:</strong> File with KIPO (Korean IP Office). South Korea is first-to-file — file early.</li>
        <li><strong>Trademark "iRESTORE":</strong> File in Class 10 (medical devices) and Class 44 (medical services). Timeline: 10–12 months. Cost: $200–$400 per class.</li>
        <li><strong>Design patents:</strong> 15-year term for device design protection.</li>
      </ul>

## Japan – PMDA/MHLW Pathway
<!-- id: reg-japan -->

<h2>Japan: PMDA Shonin Approval for Hair Growth Devices</h2>

      <h3>Classification & Approval Bodies</h3>
      <p>Japan has a <strong>dual authority structure</strong>: MHLW (Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare) sets policy, while PMDA (Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency) conducts technical reviews. Hair growth laser/LED devices are classified as <strong>medical devices</strong> — likely Class II or III depending on JMDN code assignment.</p>
      <p>Most likely pathway: <strong>Shonin (Approval)</strong> via PMDA review + MHLW administrative approval. This is significantly more involved than South Korea's pathway.</p>

      <h3>Shonin Approval Process</h3>
      <table>
        <tr><th>Step</th><th>Description</th><th>Timeline</th><th>Cost (USD)</th></tr>
        <tr><td>1</td><td><strong>Foreign Manufacturer Registration (FMR)</strong> — register with MHLW</td><td>2–4 weeks</td><td>$0</td></tr>
        <tr><td>2</td><td><strong>Designate DMAH</strong> — appoint Japanese entity as marketing authorization holder</td><td>2–4 weeks</td><td>Commission-based (15–25% of sales)</td></tr>
        <tr><td>3</td><td><strong>Pre-Submission Consultation with PMDA</strong> — confirm classification, clinical data needs, pathway</td><td>4–8 weeks</td><td>$700–$2,000</td></tr>
        <tr><td>4</td><td><strong>STED Preparation & Translation</strong> — comprehensive dossier in Japanese</td><td>8–16 weeks</td><td>$10,000–$25,000</td></tr>
        <tr><td>5</td><td><strong>Clinical Data Assessment</strong> — PMDA may accept foreign data or require Japanese trials</td><td>0–52 weeks</td><td>$0 – $300,000</td></tr>
        <tr><td>6</td><td><strong>Shonin Application to PMDA</strong></td><td>1–2 weeks</td><td>$10,000 (application fee)</td></tr>
        <tr><td>7</td><td><strong>PMDA Technical Review</strong> — includes deficiency letter rounds (avg 1–3 Q&A cycles)</td><td>9–12 months</td><td>$5,000–$15,000 (consultant)</td></tr>
        <tr><td>8</td><td><strong>MHLW Administrative Approval</strong></td><td>1–2 months</td><td>$0</td></tr>
        <tr class="highlight-row"><td></td><td><strong>TOTAL (no clinical trials)</strong></td><td><strong>12–14 months</strong></td><td><strong>$37,000–$95,000</strong></td></tr>
        <tr style="background:#fff3e0"><td></td><td><strong>TOTAL (with Japanese trials)</strong></td><td><strong>24–36 months</strong></td><td><strong>$100,000–$435,000</strong></td></tr>
      </table>

      <div class="tip"><strong>Critical Decision Point:</strong> Whether PMDA requires Japanese clinical trials is the single biggest variable. The pre-submission consultation (Step 3) answers this question. Strategy: leverage FDA 510(k) clinical data + argue gap assessment shows Japanese population data isn't needed.</div>

      <h3>DMAH vs. MAH — Local Partner Structure</h3>
      <table>
        <tr><th>Aspect</th><th>DMAH (Recommended)</th><th>MAH</th></tr>
        <tr><td>Ownership of approval</td><td>Foreign manufacturer retains</td><td>Transferred to Japanese entity</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Change flexibility</td><td>Easy (1–2 months to switch)</td><td>Difficult (6–12 months, reapproval needed)</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Cost</td><td>Lower (agent commission)</td><td>Higher (ownership/management fees)</td></tr>
        <tr><td>Recommendation</td><td><strong>Better for most cases</strong></td><td>Only if seeking Japan-based ownership</td></tr>
      </table>

      <h3>Key Differences from FDA</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Harder:</strong> Clinical data more likely required, longer review (9–16 months vs. FDA 90 days for 510(k)), all docs must be in Japanese, DMAH mandatory, stricter advertising claims</li>
        <li><strong>Easier:</strong> Lower application fee ($10K vs. FDA PMA $235K), accepts foreign clinical data with gap assessment, no separate GMP system (uses ISO 13485)</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>E-Commerce in Japan</h3>
      <p><strong>Amazon Japan</strong> is the most accessible platform for foreign sellers. Rakuten Ichiba is more restrictive (requires Japanese company registration). All platforms require PMDA approval number on listings, Japanese-language customer support, and DMAH contact information.</p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Amazon Japan:</strong> Professional seller account, 8–15% commission, accessible to foreign sellers</li>
        <li><strong>Rakuten:</strong> Requires Japanese company or service partner, 20–35% distributor commission</li>
        <li><strong>Recommended:</strong> Hybrid approach — direct Japanese website for premium positioning + Amazon Japan for volume</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Post-Market Surveillance (Fuguai Reporting)</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Serious adverse events (death/serious injury): report within <strong>10 days</strong> to PMDA</li>
        <li>Other adverse events: report within <strong>30 days</strong></li>
        <li>Full investigation report with root cause analysis within <strong>60 days</strong></li>
        <li>Annual safety reports required for marketed devices</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>IP Protection</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Patents:</strong> File with JPO (Japan Patent Office). First-to-file system. 20-year term.</li>
        <li><strong>Trademark "iRESTORE":</strong> File in Class 10 + Class 44. Timeline: 6–12 months. Cost: $300–$600.</li>
        <li><strong>Madrid Protocol:</strong> Can file U.S. trademark as basis for international filing covering Japan — cheaper and faster than direct filing.</li>
      </ul>
