**Date:** March 10, 2026
**Scenario:** Sub-$200 wand (Skincare device) - "More retail friendly" per Kevin

| Item | Value | Notes |

## CHANNEL-BY-CHANNEL BREAKDOWN

```
Retail Price:              $199.00

  - DTC Packaging         ($3.00)

  - Payment Processing    ($5.97)  [3% of $199]

GROSS MARGIN:              52.3%
```

**Note:** DTC has highest margin but lowest volume. Includes allocation for customer service, returns, chargeback risk.

### 3. COSTCO

```
Your Net Revenue:          $176.32  [Costco takes 12% = $22.68]
  - COGS (Exclusive SKU)  ($62.00)  [+$2 for custom color/packaging]
  - Retail Packaging      ($4.50)
  - Costco Fulfillment    ($4.00)   [Pallet-level efficiency]
  - Costco Demo Cost      ($3.00)   [In-warehouse sampling amortized]

  ─────────────────────────────────
GROSS PROFIT:              $93.32
GROSS MARGIN:              52.9%
```

**Strategic Value:**
- HIGHEST VOLUME (15% of revenue = ~$13.5M on $90M base)
- Exclusive SKU (SMU strategy) = no Amazon/DTC price conflict
- "Stamp of trust" halo effect to other channels
- Efficient fulfillment (pallet model)

**Key Economics:** SMU adds $2 COGS but protects channel pricing. Justifiable trade-off.

### 4. SEPHORA

```
Wholesale Price:           $99.50   [50% retailer margin; negotiated]
  - COGS                  ($60.00)
  - Retail Packaging      ($4.50)
  - Co-op/MDF Allocation  ($10.00)  [10% of wholesale = marketing investment]
  - Sales/Broker Fee      ($5.00)   [5% of wholesale; broker commission]
  - Return Reserve        ($2.00)   [Sephora returns: 60 days]
  - Overhead Allocation   ($3.00)
  ─────────────────────────────────
GROSS PROFIT:              $15.00
GROSS MARGIN:              15.1%
```

**Margin Crisis:**
- Sephora's 50% margin requirement leaves you only $99.50 wholesale
- After packaging, co-op, broker, returns = **$15 gross profit per unit**
- **This is a BRAND BUILDING play, not a margin play**

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Gateway to skincare ecosystem (consumables at 3.4x absorption via electroporation)
- Builds brand credibility for exit narrative (omnichannel, not just DTC/Amazon)
- Volume potential: If you get 100 Sephora stores × 10 units/month = 12K units/year × $15 = $180K annual contribution
- Better economics if bundled with serum/accessories

**Key Question for Kevin:** "Are we comfortable with $15 unit margin at Sephora if the strategic value is brand positioning for exit?"

### 5. ULTA BEAUTY

```
Wholesale Price:           $99.50   [50% retailer margin; standard]
  - COGS                  ($60.00)
  - Retail Packaging      ($4.50)
  - Co-op/MDF Allocation  ($10.00)  [10% of wholesale]
  - Sales/Broker Fee      ($5.00)   [5% of wholesale]
  - EDI/System Costs      ($0.75)   [3rd party EDI monthly amortized]
  - Return Reserve        ($2.00)   [Ulta: 30-60 day returns]
  - Overhead Allocation   ($3.00)
  ─────────────────────────────────
GROSS PROFIT:              $14.25
GROSS MARGIN:             14.3%
```

**Comparison vs. Sephora:**
- **Same margin profile** (both at 50% retailer take)
- **Ulta = higher volume potential** (44M loyalty members vs. Sephora's smaller prestige audience)
- **Ulta = stricter operations** (EDI required 30 days, higher fill rate SLAs)
- **Net:** Similar unit margin, but Ulta volume compensates

**Volume Estimate:** If Ulta gives you 200 stores × 8 units/month = 19K units/year × $14.25 = $271K annual contribution (better than Sephora)

### 6. WALMART (or TARGET)

```
Wholesale Price:           $119.40  [40% retailer margin; negotiated]
  - COGS                  ($60.00)
  - Retail Packaging      ($4.50)
  - Anti-Theft Tagging    ($1.50)  [High-value item requirement]
  - Co-op/MDF Allocation  ($5.97)  [5% of wholesale for launch support]
  - Sales/Broker Fee      ($6.00)  [5% of wholesale]
  - Chargebacks/Returns   ($2.00)  [Walmart chargebacks are aggressive]
  - Overhead Allocation   ($4.00)
  ─────────────────────────────────
GROSS PROFIT:              $35.43
GROSS MARGIN:             29.7%
```

**Why Walmart is Better than Sephora/Ulta:**
- 40% margin (vs. 50%) leaves you $119.40 wholesale
- After all costs, you get ~$35 per unit
- **2.3x the margin of Sephora/Ulta**

**Volume Potential (Target: 500-store test):**
- 500 stores × 15 units/month (retail expectation) = 7,500 units/month
- $35 × 7,500 = **$262.5K/month gross profit from Walmart alone**
- Annualized: ~$3.15M gross profit (on ~$3.6M revenue)

**But This Is Capital Intensive:**
- Inventory position: 500 stores × 15 units × 2-3 weeks stock = 5,250 units in retail
- At $60 COGS = $315K in retail inventory
- Plus packaging, marketing, demo staffing = $100K+ in co-op budget

**Key Economics Insight:** Walmart/Target volume justifies the lower margin, but requires serious working capital.

## SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS: WHAT IF?

### What if COGS increases to $70 (10% inflation)?

| Channel | Original GP/unit | New GP/unit | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| DTC | $104 | $94 | -10% margin |
| Sephora | $15 | $5 | **-67% margin** ⚠️ BREAKS |
| Ulta | $14.25 | $4.25 | **-70% margin** ⚠️ BREAKS |
| Walmart | $35.43 | $25.43 | -28% margin (survivable) |

**Key Insight:** Sephora/Ulta unit economics are fragile at current COGS levels. Must either:
1. Negotiate higher wholesale prices (unlikely)
2. Drive bundle sales (device + serum)
3. Accept that early-stage retail is a brand investment, not profit center

---

### What if Walmart scales to 2,000 stores (vs. 500-store test)?

```
500 stores:  7,500 units/month  × $35 = $262.5K/month GP
1,000 stores: 15,000 units/month × $35 = $525K/month GP
2,000 stores: 30,000 units/month × $35 = $1.05M/month GP
```

**Working Capital Impact:**
- 2,000 stores × 30 days supply = 30K units in retail
- At $60 COGS = $1.8M inventory (vs. $315K at 500 stores)
- Requires financing or cash generation from other channels

---

### What if Bundle Strategy (Device + Serum) Succeeds?

```
Unbundled (Sephora):
  Wholesale: $99.50 → Gross Profit: $15

Bundled (Device $120 + Serum $79.99 = $199.99):
  Wholesale Bundle: $140  [Retailer takes 30% on bundle]
  - COGS Device:    ($60)
  - COGS Serum:     ($10) [High-margin consumable]
  - Packaging:      ($5)
  - Co-op:          ($15)
  ─────────────────────────
  Gross Profit:     $50
```

**Bundle Changes the Math:** $50 bundle GP vs. $15 device-only. **This is the key to Sephora/Ulta viability.**

## CFO DECISION TREE

### Question 1: Can we sustain retail margins?
**Answer:** Only with bundles (device + serum). Device-only @ Sephora/Ulta is brand investment, not profit.

### Question 2: What's the payback on retail investment?
**Answer:** 12-18 months if volume ramps as targeted ($262.5K/month from Walmart alone by month 6).

### Question 3: Do we have working capital for retail inventory?
**Answer:** Need ~$400-500K in WC for Walmart 500-store test. Achievable from Costco/Amazon cash flow.

### Question 4: What margin should we target by exit?
**Answer:** 45-50% blended (down from 65% today). Retail diversification justifies compression for acquiring buyer.

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

### Tier 1 Channels (Launch Year 1)
1. **Costco** — Already generating 15% of revenue. Maintain and deepen (SMU strategy). **No additional investment needed.**
2. **Amazon** — Core 60-70% of revenue. Optimize bundle strategy. **Ongoing investment only.**
3. **Walmart/Target** — Launch 500-store test. Target $500K/month by month 6. **$500K investment, 12-month payback.**

### Tier 2 Channels (Launch if Serum Ready)
4. **Sephora** — Only if bundle strategy proven. Position as gateway to skincare. **$300K co-op budget Year 1.**
5. **Ulta** — Higher volume than Sephora due to loyalty program. Launch after serum validation. **$300K co-op budget Year 1.**

### Tier 3 Channels (Strategic Reserve)
6. **Nordstrom** — Small volume, high brand halo. Launch if Sephora/Ulta validation needed. **$100K co-op budget.**
7. **QVC/HSN** — Use as working capital reserve, not primary channel. **No fixed budget; ad-hoc.**

- **Revenue Mix:** DTC 20%, Amazon 40%, Costco 15%, Retail (Walmart/Target/Sephora/Ulta) 25%
- **Blended Gross Margin:** 50% (down from 65%, but risk-reduced portfolio)
- **Exit Multiple Uplift:** Multi-channel reduces buyer risk, potentially 20-30% higher valuation than pure-Amazon business
