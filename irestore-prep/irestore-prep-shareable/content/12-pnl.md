## Lever: Returns (10% → <8%)
<!-- id: pnl-returns -->

<h3>Returns & Allowances — estimated ~10% of gross / $10.5M</h3>
<div class="tip"><strong>Key insight:</strong> Current estimated return rate is 10–12%; industry best-in-class is &lt;8%. At ~$105M gross, each 1pp of return reduction is worth <strong>$1.1M</strong> straight to the bottom line. Reducing to &lt;8% would unlock <strong>$2.1–4.2M</strong> in incremental EBITDA.</div>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Improve unboxing & setup experience:</strong> Most device returns come from confusion or unmet expectations. Create video QR codes in packaging for first-use walkthrough.</li>
  <li><strong>"Save the Sale" retention team:</strong> Before processing a return, route to a specialist who troubleshoots. Many device returns are "I don't know if it's working" — addressable with education.</li>
  <li><strong>Pre-purchase expectation setting:</strong> Add "Results timeline" content (visible in 3–6 months) to listings and DTC. Mismatched expectations are a top return driver.</li>
  <li><strong>Return reason analysis:</strong> Categorize every return by reason code. "Didn't work" vs. "didn't see results" vs. "wrong product" vs. "defective" each require different interventions.</li>
  <li><strong>Channel-specific return rates:</strong> Amazon vs. DTC vs. Costco return rates are likely very different. Costco's generous return policy could be inflating the blended number.</li>
</ul>

## Lever: COGS (33% → 30%)
<!-- id: pnl-cogs -->

<h3>Product Manufacturing — currently ~25% of net rev / $22.5M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Volume-based renegotiation:</strong> At $90M+, even 2–3% improvement on component pricing yields $450K–675K in savings. Periodic RFQ benchmarking against 2–3 alternative suppliers maintains competitive pricing.</li>
  <li><strong>Value engineering:</strong> Systematic review of component specifications to identify simplifications that don't affect product efficacy. LED driver boards, housing materials, and connectors are typical targets.</li>
  <li><strong>SKU rationalization:</strong> Fewer SKUs = longer production runs = lower per-unit manufacturing cost. Analysis of SKU productivity can identify bottom performers for potential discontinuation.</li>
  <li><strong>Manufacturing relationship advantage:</strong> Current Shenzhen facility partnership with net-150 payment terms provides both cost and cash flow advantages. Maintaining this relationship while formalizing procurement processes optimizes both factors.</li>
</ul>

<!-- id: pnl-freight -->

<h3>Inbound Freight & Duties — currently ~6% / $5.4M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Container utilization:</strong> Optimize pallet configs and carton dimensions. 5% better utilization = $270K savings.</li>
  <li><strong>Duty engineering:</strong> Review HTS classification. First Sale valuation can reduce dutiable value.</li>
  <li><strong>Nearshoring for top SKUs:</strong> Evaluate Mexico assembly for 2–3 fastest-moving SKUs. Reduces transit from 35 days to 5, may reduce/eliminate duties under USMCA.</li>

## Lever: Amazon Fees (11.5%)
<!-- id: pnl-amazon -->

<h3>Amazon Fees — currently ~11.5% / $10.4M (referral $4.5M + FBA $5.9M)</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Shift volume to DTC:</strong> Every $1M moved from Amazon to DTC saves ~$230K in combined referral + FBA fees, minus the cost of DTC fulfillment and payment processing (~$130K). Net ~$100K improvement per $1M shifted, before marketing costs.</li>
  <li><strong>FBA inventory optimization:</strong> Just-in-time replenishment to avoid long-term storage fees. Keep IPI score high.</li>
  <li><strong>Seller Fulfilled Prime:</strong> For oversized or slow-moving SKUs, SFP avoids FBA fees while keeping Prime badge.</li>
  <li><strong>Amazon 60–70% of category:</strong> With dominant market share, iRESTORE has negotiating leverage for co-op advertising credits and promotional placement.</li>
</ul>

## Lever: People (9%)
<!-- id: pnl-people -->

<h3>People — currently ~9% / $8.1M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Headcount:</strong> ~80 FTE + 50 overseas + contractors = 100+ total. ~$81K avg loaded cost per FTE equivalent.</li>
  <li><strong>Nearshore Tier 1 CS:</strong> Move basic CS (order status, simple troubleshooting) to nearshore team (Mexico, Colombia). Keep Tier 2 onshore. Typical savings: 40–60% on CS labor.</li>
  <li><strong>Performance-based comp:</strong> Align bonuses to contribution margin, not just revenue, to prevent growth-at-any-cost behavior.</li>
  <li><strong>EOS structure:</strong> ~60% adopted per Kevin. Full rollout creates accountability without adding headcount.</li>
</ul>

## Lever: Fulfillment (3%)
<!-- id: pnl-fulfillment -->

<h3>DTC Fulfillment & Shipping — currently ~3% / $2.7M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>East Coast fulfillment node:</strong> Reduces avg shipping zone from 5–6 to 2–3. Typical savings: 15–25% on outbound shipping.</li>
  <li><strong>Annual 3PL benchmarking:</strong> Even $0.25/order improvement = significant savings on DTC volume.</li>
  <li><strong>Right-sized packaging:</strong> Custom packaging to minimize dimensional weight surcharges. 10–15% reduction in DIM weight charges.</li>
</ul>

## Lever: Technology (1.8%)
<!-- id: pnl-tech -->

<h3>Technology & Software — currently ~1.8% / $1.6M</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Software rationalization:</strong> Systematic audit to identify overlapping tools and consolidation opportunities. Fast-growing companies typically achieve 15–20% savings through tool consolidation.</li>
  <li><strong>Analytics infrastructure:</strong> Implement data warehouse and BI layer to enable real-time visibility into margin drivers by channel and SKU. Currently on Fulfil.io ERP.</li>
  <li><strong>Process automation:</strong> Identify repetitive operational tasks for automation — CS workflows, demand forecasting, dynamic pricing, and reporting are common candidates for efficiency gains.</li>
</ul>
