## Product Portfolio
<!-- id: company-products -->

<div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Hair Growth Devices (Core)</div>
        <p>FDA-cleared low-level laser therapy (LLLT) helmets and caps. Multiple price points from ~$600 to $1,400+. This is the category where they dominate with 60-70% Amazon market share. Clinical studies supporting efficacy. Macro tailwind: influencers (Bryan Johnson, Huberman) driving massive organic demand for hair growth.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Skincare Devices (Growth Engine)</div>
        <p>LED face masks, skincare wands, and related devices. Newer category but massive TAM — skincare is 20x the addressable market of hair therapy. This is where the long-term growth story lives. Competitors in this space include CurrentBody, Dr. Dennis Gross, Omnilux, and FOREO.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card">

## Structural Advantages
<!-- id: company-advantages -->

<ul>
        <li><span class="label">Vertical integration:</span> Father's Shenzhen factory provides manufacturing at cost with net-150 payment terms. This creates a negative cash conversion cycle — iRESTORE gets paid by customers before they pay the factory.</li>
        <li><span class="label">Category dominance:</span> 60–70% Amazon market share in hair growth. Nearest competitor (Capillus) is 4–5x smaller. This provides pricing power and organic demand capture.</li>
        <li><span class="label">Macro tailwind:</span> Hair growth and at-home beauty devices are riding a massive cultural wave. Influencer mentions have shifted the business from "educating and creating demand" to "capturing demand."</li>
        <li><span class="label">FDA clearance:</span> Regulatory moat that takes competitors 1–3 years to replicate.</li>
        <li><span class="label">Profitable growth:</span> Unlike many DTC brands, iRESTORE is profitable and growing 65–80% YoY. Strong unit economics.</li>
      </ul>
