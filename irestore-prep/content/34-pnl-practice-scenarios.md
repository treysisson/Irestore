## Scenario A: New SKU Launch — Go or No-Go?
<!-- id: scenario-a -->

<div class="tip"><strong>Setup:</strong> You run a DTC hair restoration device company doing $85M/yr. Your product team wants to launch a new red-light body panel device at $349. The CEO is excited. Finance built the projection below. You have 30 minutes to decide whether to green-light it.</div>

<h3>New SKU: Year 1 Projection</h3>
<table>
  <tr><th>Line Item</th><th>Amount</th><th>Notes</th></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Gross Revenue</strong></td><td>$8.4M</td><td>24K units @ $349</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Returns & Allowances</td><td>($0.8M)</td><td>9.5% return rate (assumed same as core SKU)</td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#e8f5e9"><td><strong>Net Revenue</strong></td><td><strong>$7.6M</strong></td><td></td></tr>
  <tr><td>COGS</td><td>($3.0M)</td><td>$126/unit — new manufacturer, no volume discount yet</td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#e8f5e9"><td><strong>Gross Profit</strong></td><td><strong>$4.6M</strong></td><td>61% gross margin</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Marketing & Launch</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">($3.8M)</td><td>Includes $600K influencer seeding + paid media</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Shipping & Fulfillment</td><td>($0.6M)</td><td></td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#e3f2fd"><td><strong>Contribution Margin</strong></td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;"><strong>$0.2M</strong></td><td>2.6% CM</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Allocated Overhead</td><td>($0.9M)</td><td>Ops, CS, tech — estimated allocation</td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#fff3e0"><td><strong>Net Income (Year 1)</strong></td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;"><strong>($0.7M)</strong></td><td></td></tr>
</table>

<h3>Year 2 Projection (if launched)</h3>
<table>
  <tr><th>Metric</th><th>Year 2 Estimate</th><th>Assumption</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Units Sold</td><td>48K</td><td>2x Year 1 on brand awareness + repeat</td></tr>
  <tr><td>COGS/unit</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$98</td><td>Volume pricing kicks in at 40K units</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Marketing % of Revenue</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">32%</td><td>Down from 50% — brand established</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Est. CM%</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">22%</td><td></td></tr>
</table>

<h3>Additional Context</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Core SKU (hair device) is currently running at <strong>31% CM</strong> in Year 3+</li>
  <li>New device requires <strong>$1.2M upfront tooling/mold investment</strong> (not in above P&L — treated as capex)</li>
  <li>Customer research shows <strong>41% of current buyers</strong> expressed interest in a body device</li>
  <li>A competitor launched a similar product 8 months ago and is selling ~3K units/month on Amazon</li>
</ul>

<h4>❓ Questions — Scenario A</h4>
<div class="card card-yellow">
<ol>
<li><strong>The Year 1 P&L shows a $700K loss. Does that mean you shouldn't launch?</strong> What's the right framework for making this decision?</li>
<li><strong>What's the biggest risk hiding in the assumptions?</strong> Pick one number in the projection that you'd push back on hardest before approving.</li>
<li><strong>The CEO is excited and the competitor is already in market. How do you present your recommendation without killing the momentum?</strong></li>
</ol>
</div>

## Scenario B: Marketing Mix Reallocation
<!-- id: scenario-b -->

<div class="tip"><strong>Setup:</strong> You're 5 months into the year. Meta CPMs are up 38% YoY. Your blended CAC has crept from $68 to $91. The CMO says "we just need to ride it out." The board wants an answer before next quarter's budget gets approved.</div>

<h3>Current Marketing Spend by Channel (YTD, Jan–May)</h3>
<table>
  <tr><th>Channel</th><th>Spend</th><th>Revenue Attributed</th><th>ROAS</th><th>New Customers</th><th>CAC</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Meta (FB/IG)</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">$5.8M</td><td>$13.9M</td><td>2.4x</td><td>38K</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">$153</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Google Search</td><td>$1.6M</td><td>$5.8M</td><td>3.6x</td><td>14K</td><td>$114</td></tr>
  <tr><td>YouTube</td><td>$0.9M</td><td>$2.1M</td><td>2.3x</td><td>7K</td><td>$129</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Influencer/Affiliate</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$1.1M</td><td>$4.8M</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">4.4x</td><td>18K</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$61</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Email/SMS</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$0.2M</td><td>$3.2M</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">16x</td><td>—</td><td>—</td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#e8f5e9"><td><strong>Total</strong></td><td><strong>$9.6M</strong></td><td><strong>$29.8M</strong></td><td><strong>3.1x</strong></td><td><strong>77K</strong></td><td><strong>$125</strong></td></tr>
</table>

<h3>Additional Context</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Product CM (before marketing) is <strong>58%</strong> — you need blended ROAS above <strong>2.0x</strong> to be profitable at the contribution level</li>
  <li>Influencer program currently has <strong>22 active creators</strong> — team says it can scale to 80+ but takes 3–4 months to recruit and onboard</li>
  <li>Email/SMS list: <strong>410K subscribers</strong>, currently sent 2x/month — deliverability is strong</li>
  <li>Q4 is your biggest quarter — <strong>40% of annual revenue</strong>. Budget decisions made now affect Q4 readiness</li>
</ul>

<h4>❓ Questions — Scenario B</h4>
<div class="card card-yellow">
<ol>
<li><strong>The blended CAC went from $68 to $125. Where is the problem, specifically?</strong> Which channel would you cut and which would you invest more in — show your reasoning from the table.</li>
<li><strong>The CMO says "ride it out." What's the business cost of doing nothing for another 90 days?</strong> Try to size it roughly.</li>
<li><strong>The influencer program has the best economics but takes 3-4 months to scale. It's May. Do you still invest in it for this year?</strong></li>
</ol>
</div>

## Scenario C: Pricing & Promotion Analysis
<!-- id: scenario-c -->

<div class="tip"><strong>Setup:</strong> Your company runs a major sale twice a year — one in July (summer promo) and one on Black Friday. Last year's Black Friday promo is below. The CFO says it was "a great success." You're being asked to approve the same playbook for this year.</div>

<h3>Black Friday Promo: Before vs. During</h3>
<table>
  <tr><th>Metric</th><th>Normal Week</th><th>BF Promo Week</th><th>BF+2 Weeks</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Units Sold</td><td>1,200</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">6,800</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">480</td></tr>
  <tr><td>ASP (avg selling price)</td><td>$249</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">$174</td><td>$249</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Gross Revenue</td><td>$299K</td><td>$1.18M</td><td>$120K</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Return Rate</td><td>6%</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">14%</td><td>7%</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Net Revenue</td><td>$281K</td><td>$1.01M</td><td>$111K</td></tr>
  <tr><td>COGS</td><td>($87K)</td><td>($493K)</td><td>($35K)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Marketing Spend</td><td>($62K)</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">($310K)</td><td>($55K)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Shipping & Fulfillment</td><td>($22K)</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">($156K)</td><td>($9K)</td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#e3f2fd"><td><strong>Contribution Margin</strong></td><td><strong>$110K</strong></td><td style="background:#fff9c4;"><strong>$57K</strong></td><td><strong>$12K</strong></td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>CM %</strong></td><td><strong>39%</strong></td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;"><strong>5.6%</strong></td><td><strong>10%</strong></td></tr>
</table>

<h3>Additional Context</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>30% discount</strong> applied site-wide during BF week ($249 → $174)</li>
  <tr></tr>
  <li>Post-promo demand dip (BF+2 weeks) is well below normal — <strong>likely cannibalization effect</strong></li>
  <li>BF promo new customer mix: <strong>68% new customers</strong> vs 32% existing — normally 45% new</li>
  <li>New customers acquired during promo have shown <strong>40% lower 12-month LTV</strong> in prior year cohort analysis</li>
</ul>

<h4>❓ Questions — Scenario C</h4>
<div class="card card-yellow">
<ol>
<li><strong>The CFO says Black Friday was "a great success." Do the numbers back that up?</strong> Walk through what actually happened to profitability across the 3-week window.</li>
<li><strong>What's the hidden cost that isn't showing up in this table at all?</strong></li>
<li><strong>Would you run the same promo this year? If not, what would you change and why?</strong></li>
</ol>
</div>

## Scenario D: Headcount Decision
<!-- id: scenario-d -->

<div class="tip"><strong>Setup:</strong> Your company just crossed $90M ARR and is projecting $155M next year. The leadership team submitted headcount requests totaling 28 new hires for H1. You have budget for 14. You need to decide who gets approved.</div>

<h3>Headcount Requests by Department</h3>
<table>
  <tr><th>Department</th><th>Requested</th><th>All-In Cost (annual)</th><th>Stated Justification</th><th>Revenue Impact</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Performance Marketing</td><td>3</td><td>$420K</td><td>Scale paid media — currently outsourced at higher CPM</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">Direct: ~$4-6M attributable revenue</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Customer Experience</td><td>5</td><td>$350K</td><td>WISMO tickets up 40%, response time at 3.1 days</td><td style="background:#fff9c4;">Indirect: retention, review scores</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Supply Chain / Ops</td><td>4</td><td>$520K</td><td>Single-threaded on logistics — one person manages all freight</td><td style="background:#fff9c4;">Risk mitigation, not growth</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Product Development</td><td>6</td><td>$840K</td><td>3 new SKUs planned for 2026 launch</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">Speculative: revenue in 18+ months</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Finance & Analytics</td><td>2</td><td>$280K</td><td>COO (you) needs better data infrastructure</td><td style="background:#fff9c4;">Indirect: better decisions</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Brand / Content</td><td>4</td><td>$460K</td><td>Influencer program needs dedicated management</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">Direct: ties to influencer ROAS improvement</td></tr>
  <tr><td>IT / Engineering</td><td>4</td><td>$560K</td><td>Website performance, app rebuild</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">Unclear: no stated metric</td></tr>
  <tr style="background:#e8f5e9"><td><strong>Total</strong></td><td><strong>28</strong></td><td><strong>$3.43M</strong></td><td></td><td></td></tr>
</table>

<h3>Current State Snapshot</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Headcount today: <strong>62 employees</strong></li>
  <li>Revenue per employee: <strong>$1.45M</strong> (industry benchmark for DTC at this stage: ~$1.0-1.2M)</li>
  <li>You are already above benchmark — adding 14 people brings you to <strong>$2.03M rev/employee</strong> at $155M projection</li>
  <li>CX ticket response time SLA: target is <strong>24 hours</strong>, currently at <strong>3.1 days</strong></li>
</ul>

<h4>❓ Questions — Scenario D</h4>
<div class="card card-yellow">
<ol>
<li><strong>You can approve 14 of the 28 requested roles. Which departments get cut and which get approved?</strong> Walk through your logic — don't just pick the cheapest ones.</li>
<li><strong>Supply Chain flagged that they're single-threaded on logistics. You cut them from 4 to 2. What risk are you taking on, and how do you manage it?</strong></li>
<li><strong>The CEO is pushing hard for the Product Development hires to hit the 3 new SKU plan. You're skeptical. How do you push back without killing the ambition?</strong></li>
</ol>
</div>

## Scenario E: International Expansion Decision
<!-- id: scenario-e -->

<div class="tip"><strong>Setup:</strong> Your US business is at $90M. The board is asking about international expansion as a path to $200M. Two markets have been analyzed: UK and Canada. You need to give a recommendation on sequencing.</div>

<h3>Market Entry Comparison</h3>
<table>
  <tr><th>Factor</th><th>UK</th><th>Canada</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Addressable Market (TAM)</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$280M</td><td>$95M</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Estimated Year 1 Revenue</td><td>$4.2M</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$3.8M</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Estimated Year 3 Revenue</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$18M</td><td>$9M</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Launch Investment Required</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">$2.8M</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">$0.9M</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Time to First Sale</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">9–12 months (regulatory)</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">3–4 months</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Regulatory Complexity</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">High (UKCA cert, new process)</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">Low (existing FDA clearance accepted)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Logistics Complexity</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">High (new 3PL, customs, VAT)</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">Low (can ship from US warehouse)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Language/Localization</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">None needed</td><td style="background:#fff9c4;">French required in Quebec</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Competitive Intensity</td><td style="background:#fff9c4;">Medium (1 strong local player)</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">Low</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Estimated Year 1 CM%</td><td style="background:#ffcdd2;">8%</td><td style="background:#c8e6c9;">19%</td></tr>
</table>

<h3>Internal Capacity Context</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Your ops team is already stretched — the logistics lead manages all US freight solo</li>
  <li>Finance has no international accounting or VAT experience</li>
  <li>The CEO has a personal connection with a UK distributor who is "very excited"</li>
  <li>Board wants to show international revenue by <strong>Q3 this year</strong></li>
</ul>

<h4>❓ Questions — Scenario E</h4>
<div class="card card-yellow">
<ol>
<li><strong>The UK has a much larger TAM and Year 3 upside. Canada is easier and faster. Which do you launch first and why?</strong> Be specific about the trade-off you're making.</li>
<li><strong>The CEO has a relationship with a UK distributor and is excited about it. How does that change — or not change — your recommendation?</strong></li>
<li><strong>The board wants international revenue by Q3. It's currently Q1. Is that achievable? What do you say if you don't think it is?</strong></li>
</ol>
</div>
