      <h3>Where iRESTORE Should Price</h3>
      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">Recommended: Professional Panel System at $5,000–$12,000</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Why not beds ($25K–$80K)?</strong> Too much engineering leap from helmets. Beds require entirely different manufacturing, installation, and support infrastructure. Leave this to TheraLight for now.</li>
          <li><strong>Why panels?</strong> iRESTORE already manufactures LED/laser devices. Scaling up to a larger panel form factor is a logical engineering extension. Their existing supply chain (likely Shenzhen/Vietnam OEM) can support this.</li>
          <li><strong>Sweet spot: $5K–$12K professional multi-panel system.</strong> Undercuts Joovv commercial ($6K–15K), significantly undercuts beds, and positions above cheap Chinese panels.</li>
          <li><strong>Key differentiator:</strong> iRESTORE has FDA clearance and 29,000+ reviews. Most panel companies do NOT have FDA clearance. This matters enormously for med spas and derm clinics.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <h3>Recurring Revenue Streams (Beyond Initial Device Sale)</h3>
      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">The B2B Consumables Flywheel</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Replacement LED panels/modules</strong> — LEDs degrade over time (~50,000 hours). Clinics running 8+ hours/day will need replacements every 2–3 years. Estimated $1,500–$3,000 per replacement.</li>
          <li><strong>Extended warranties / service contracts</strong> — $500–$1,500/year. Clinics value uptime guarantees.</li>
          <li><strong>Consumable referral program</strong> — sell iRESTORE topicals (serums, shampoos) through clinic partners at wholesale. Clinics resell at retail markup. Builds iRESTORE brand in professional channel.</li>
          <li><strong>Co-branded marketing materials</strong> — provide clinics with patient education materials that drive home-use device sales (consumer upsell from B2B relationship).</li>
        </ul>
        <div class="tip"><strong>The strategic play:</strong> Every B2B placement is a billboard for consumer products. A patient who uses iRESTORE’s professional panel at their dermatologist’s office is the perfect lead for a $695 home helmet. This is how you build a consumer-professional ecosystem like Olaplex did (salon → at-home).</div>
      </div>

## B2B Impact on Exit Valuation
<!-- id: b2b-exit -->

<h2>Why B2B Matters for the Exit Story</h2>

      <div class="card">
        <div class="card-header">B2B Revenue Makes iRESTORE More Acquirable</div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Revenue diversification</strong> — reduces dependence on Amazon and DTC. Acquirers hate single-channel risk. B2B adds a third revenue pillar.</li>
          <li><strong>Higher margins</strong> — ~60% gross margin vs. ~30% on Amazon. Improves blended margin profile, which directly increases EBITDA and exit multiple.</li>
          <li><strong>Recurring revenue potential</strong> — service contracts and consumable resales create predictable revenue streams. Acquirers pay premium multiples for recurring revenue.</li>
          <li><strong>Professional credibility</strong> — “Used by 2,000+ dermatologists and med spas” is a powerful consumer marketing claim that reinforces the home device brand.</li>
          <li><strong>Strategic acquirer interest</strong> — medical device companies (Lumenis, Cutera, InMode) actively acquire professional aesthetic brands. A B2B line opens up a whole new category of potential acquirers beyond beauty/consumer.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
